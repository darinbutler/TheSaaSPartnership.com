Why SaaS SEO is different

Buyer journey is long and research-heavy

Content must span every stage. Awareness → Consideration → Evaluation → Decision. Each stage needs different content and messaging.

Multiple ICPs search differently

VP Sales, CMO, CTO all search for different solutions. Your SEO strategy needs separate content for each persona and their search intent.

PLG adds complexity

Self-serve SaaS integrates help docs, trial pages and community into SEO. Your strategy has to cover product-led paths alongside traditional sales funnel.

Common SaaS SEO mistakes

Targeting only product name

Nobody searches for you until they know you exist. You need category, alternative and pain-point keywords.

Top-of-funnel content with no conversion

Blog posts with no demo CTA. Educational content with no pipeline tie. SEO traffic that doesn't convert is wasted bandwidth.

Ignoring technical issues

Average SaaS site has 47 technical SEO problems. These prevent Google from ranking you. Fix the foundation first.

Content without keyword strategy

Publishing 50 blog posts without targeting keywords. That's zero search traffic. Every piece needs a keyword foundation.

No organic pipeline measurement

Treating SEO as a vanity metric. You have to track organic traffic to pipeline to prove SEO works.

The SEO content strategy

Keyword tiers by intent

Not all keywords are created equal. We segment your target keywords into tiers: Awareness (what is X?), Consideration (X vs Y), Evaluation (best X software, X alternatives), and Decision (X pricing, X reviews). Each tier gets different content and messaging.

Product-market fit keywords

The keywords where your product has unfair advantage. These are the ones where your solution genuinely solves the problem better than alternatives. These become your content pillars — deep, authoritative content that locks in rankings.

High-intent landing pages

Content isn't a blog. Every piece targets specific keywords, ranks for them, and converts them. Product comparison pages, alternative pages, category pages — all built to move buyers closer to pipeline.

Authority through topical clusters

Build topical authority around your core buyer problems. If "mobile CRM" is a key topic, create 10–15 pieces around it. Google rewards topical authority with better rankings and SERP real estate.

Technical SEO: The foundation

Core Web Vitals

Largest Contentful Paint (LCP), First Input Delay (FID), Cumulative Layout Shift (CLS). These are ranking factors. Slow sites don't rank. We optimize for speed across all metrics.

Site architecture & crawlability

Clear hierarchy, logical internal linking, no crawl errors. Google needs to easily understand your site structure to index all content.

Schema & structured data

FAQPage, SoftwareApplication, Organization, FAQs. Proper schema helps Google understand your content and show rich snippets in search results.

Indexation & robots

XML sitemaps, robots.txt, meta robots. Make sure Google can find and index all your important pages without crawling waste or duplicates.

Why organic pipeline compounds

Organic traffic is compounding. Month 1, you start at zero. Month 3, you see small gains. Month 6, momentum builds. Month 12, your organic programme is 30–45% of pipeline. Month 24, it's your strongest channel.

This is why B2B SaaS companies that do SEO properly early win long-term. Your organic programme becomes your moat. It drives pipeline, it builds trust, it compounds over years.
